Data Guide

Not sure what to enter? Here is how to find these numbers for your space.

🌳 Green Space & Biodiversity
  • Number of Trees: Total mature trees on the property.
    How to find: Physically count them or check landscape architectural plans.
  • Number of Species: The variety of different plants/trees.
    How to find: Ask your local gardener or estimate the distinct types of foliage.
  • Area: Total physical green space.
    How to find: Check property deeds, facility maps, or use the measure tool on Google Maps.
  • Project Duration: How long the green space has been established.
    How to find: Check planting or property acquisition records.
⚡ Energy & Built Environment
  • Electricity Consumption: Total annual power used.
    How to find: Check your monthly electricity bills. Add up the 'kWh' for the last 12 months.
  • Renewable Electricity: Solar or wind power generated/purchased.
    How to find: Check your solar inverter app or green tariff details on your utility bill.
  • Power Rating: Maximum power of specific equipment.
    How to find: Look at the compliance sticker on the back of appliances (look for 'Watts' or 'kW').
  • Operating Hours: Daily usage of your space/equipment.
    How to find: Estimate based on standard office hours (e.g., 8-10 hours) or home occupancy.
💧 Water Use & Management
  • Water Withdrawal: Total fresh water brought into the facility.
    How to find: Check municipal water bills (often listed in Litres, cubic meters, or Gallons).
  • Water Discharged: Water sent into the sewage system.
    How to find: Usually equal to your withdrawal minus any water used purely for landscaping/gardens.
  • Water Reused: Rainwater harvested or greywater recycled.
    How to find: Check the capacity of your rainwater harvesting tanks.
  • Site Area: Total footprint of your property.
    How to find: Real estate documents, lease agreements, or Google Maps.
♻️ Waste & Circularity
  • Hazardous Waste: Batteries, e-waste, chemicals, paints.
    How to find: Check disposal receipts from specialized e-waste or chemical recycling vendors.
  • Non-Hazardous Waste: Regular everyday garbage.
    How to find: Weigh a typical daily trash bag, multiply by days open per year. Or check municipal waste manifests.
  • Waste Recycled: Paper, plastic, cardboard, glass.
    How to find: Estimate the percentage of your waste that goes into the blue/green recycling bins.
  • Waste Sent to Landfill: Trash not recycled or composted.
    How to find: Subtract your recycled waste from your total non-hazardous waste.
