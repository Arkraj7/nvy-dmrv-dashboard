About Nagar Van Yojana (NVY)

The Nagar Van Yojana is a visionary initiative launched by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEF&CC) with the goal of creating 1,000 Urban Forests (Nagar Vans) and Urban Gardens (Nagar Vatikas) across India. These urban forests act as essential "green lungs" for cities, mitigating the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect, creating localized carbon sinks, and protecting native biodiversity.

About this DMRV Study

This Digital Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification (DMRV) platform is a robust research initiative designed to evaluate the true on-ground impact of these urban forests. By combining geospatial mapping, AI verification, and Participatory Rural Appraisal (PRA), this study quantifies ecological benefits (like air quality and groundwater recharge) alongside socio-economic impacts (community well-being and vendor livelihoods), bridging the gap between local climate action and national NDCs.

नगर वन योजना (NVY) के बारे में

नगर वन योजना पर्यावरण, वन और जलवायु परिवर्तन मंत्रालय (MoEF&CC) द्वारा शुरू की गई एक दूरदर्शी पहल है, जिसका उद्देश्य पूरे भारत में 1,000 'नगर वन' और 'नगर वाटिका' विकसित करना है। ये शहरी वन शहरों के लिए आवश्यक "ग्रीन लंग्स" (हरे फेफड़े) के रूप में कार्य करते हैं, जो अर्बन हीट आइलैंड (UHI) प्रभाव को कम करते हैं, कार्बन सिंक बनाते हैं और स्थानीय जैव विविधता की रक्षा करते हैं।

इस DMRV अध्ययन के बारे में

यह डिजिटल मॉनिटरिंग, रिपोर्टिंग और सत्यापन (DMRV) प्लेटफॉर्म एक मजबूत शोध पहल है जिसे इन शहरी वनों के वास्तविक जमीनी प्रभाव का मूल्यांकन करने के लिए डिज़ाइन किया गया है। भू-स्थानिक (Geospatial) मैपिंग, एआई (AI) सत्यापन और सहभागी ग्रामीण मूल्यांकन (PRA) को मिलाकर, यह अध्ययन पारिस्थितिक लाभों (जैसे वायु गुणवत्ता और भूजल पुनर्भरण) के साथ-साथ सामाजिक-आर्थिक प्रभावों का परिमाणीकरण (quantification) करता है, जो स्थानीय जलवायु कार्रवाई और राष्ट्रीय लक्ष्यों (NDCs) के बीच की खाई को पाटता है।

MoEF&CC Operational Framework for Nagar Van

Funding Mechanism

Supported by the National Compensatory Afforestation Fund Management and Planning Authority (CAMPA) to ensure sustainable financial backing for initial establishment and fencing.

Collaborative Governance (PPP Model)

Implementation thrives on a Public-Private Partnership involving State Forest Departments, Urban Local Bodies (ULBs), NGOs, Corporate CSR funds, and local citizens.

Land Utilization

Primarily developed on degraded forest lands or vacant lands offered by local bodies within urban and peri-urban limits.

About This Study

This study is comprehensive to understand the essence of Nagar Vans and Nagar Vatikas. It aims to evaluate their true impact on urban ecology, biodiversity, and community well-being, driving grassroots policy change and promoting sustainable urban environments.

Metrics Importance & Formulas

Live Survey Samples:
Real-time count of inputs reflecting active community and field-level engagement.
Air Quality (PM2.5):
Measures fine particulate matter mitigation. Vital for assessing public health benefits of urban forests.
Carbon Sequestration:
Formula: Biomass × 0.47. Indicates the total CO₂ equivalent absorbed, crucial for climate action goals.
Urban Heat Reduction:
Formula: ΔT (°C) Local vs Surrounding. Highlights micro-climate cooling to combat Urban Heat Islands.
Water Recharged:
Formula: Catchment Area × Rainfall × Runoff Coefficient. Shows contribution to local water tables.
Ecological Index:
Composite scale (0-10) assessing native tree density, species richness, and habitat quality.
Socio-Economic:
Evaluates non-timber forest product utility, eco-tourism potential, and vendor livelihoods generated.
Avg. WTP (CVM):
Contingent Valuation Method. The average monetary value citizens are willing to pay for maintenance.

About Nagar Van DMRV Portal

The Nagar Van DMRV (Digital Monitoring, Reporting & Verification) Dashboard is an AI-powered, geospatial platform designed to track and evaluate the ecological, infrastructural, and socio-economic impacts of India's urban forests.

By providing real-time metrics on carbon sequestration, urban cooling, air quality, and community well-being, it bridges grassroots conservation efforts with national climate policy and sustainable development goals.
